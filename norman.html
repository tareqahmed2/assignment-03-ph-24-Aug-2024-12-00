Norman Foster is a renowned British architect, born in 1935, known for his innovative and sustainable designs that have shaped modern architecture. He founded Foster + Partners, a global architecture firm, in 1967. Foster's notable works include the Gherkin in London, the Millau Viaduct in France, and the Reichstag in Berlin. His designs often incorporate high-tech elements and a deep commitment to environmental sustainability. Foster has been awarded numerous accolades, including the prestigious Pritzker Architecture Prize. His work continues to influence contemporary architecture, blending aesthetics with functionality and sustainability.
